Why do we do early market research?

There are a number of reasons we conduct early market research, and understanding these will help you to have a smooth procurement process. Explore the ideas below.

  • Technology is evolving at an increasingly fast pace, and the ICT industry is a rapidly changing landscape. When we work on large-scale or high-investment ICT projects products and solutions, it’s important that we speak to people and companies who work in the area we’re interested in.  This help us ensure we are getting the most up-to-date information to make informed decisions about how we will reach our outcomes and be informed buyers when choosing sellers.

  • By engaging with industry at an early stage, we can be more strategic and systematic in our dealings with sellers and the industry as a whole, rather than just engaging with them on a more short-term, program-by-program basis. This benefits whole programs and not just individual projects.

  • A key strategic aspect of early market research is that it can help agencies express desired outcomes and translate them into high-level statements of business requirements that are well aligned with the industry, making it much more likely that the desired outcomes can be achieved. Early market research is an important dialogue between your agency and industry, aimed at resolving the content of your requirement.

  • If we are outcomes focused and flexible over the content of our requirement, then we can offer industry the opportunity to propose previously unknown and possibly innovative solutions.

    Conversely, if it turns out that there isn’t an industry solution to our need, then we will have informed information to revisit our requirements and scope.  We can do this with minimal cost and effort if we have done our market research early.

  • Early market research allows you to access rich sources of data and information from industry on the way a particular market operates, the types of services and products available, industry pricing structures and any future changes to the industry. It also gives us a deeper understanding of a particular category of goods or services — leading to the identification of initiatives aligned to business value drivers and value-for-money outcomes.

  • This early process supports the development of robust procurement documentation relevant to the targeted industry, reducing the need for addenda or post-tender clarifications later.
